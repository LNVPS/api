Article 1 - Definitions

Agreement: This Independent IP Address Assignment and Maintenance Agreement.

Administration Fee: The one-time fee payable by the End User to the Provider for processing the initial request for Assignment.

Assignment: The act by which the RIPE NCC enables the End User to use Independent Internet Number Resources for its internal use, which may involve publication in the RIPE Database of the End User as the assignee of the respective Internet Number Resources.

End User: A natural person or legal entity that has entered into this Agreement to receive Internet Number Resources for internal purposes.

Independent Internet Number Resources: Internet Number Resources including but not limited to:

  • Provider Independent (PI) IPv4 addresses
  • Provider Independent (PI) IPv6 addresses
  • Autonomous System (AS) Numbers
  • Internet Exchange Point (IXP) assignments
  • Anycasting assignments

Maintenance Fee: The periodic fee payable by the End User to the Provider for maintaining Assignments and handling ongoing administrative requirements.

Provider: {{provider_trading_name}}, acting as a Local Internet Registry (LIR) that receives allocations for Internet Number Resources from the RIPE NCC.

Reverse DNS Delegation: Reverse Domain Name System (DNS) delegations allowing applications to map to a domain name from an IP address, using the special domain names in-addr.arpa (IPv4) and ip6.arpa (IPv6).

RIPE Database: The database operated by the RIPE NCC containing IP addresses, Autonomous System (AS) Numbers, and organisations or customers associated with these resources, along with related Points of Contact (POC).

RIPE NCC: Réseaux IP Européens Network Coordination Centre, a membership association under Dutch law established in Amsterdam, the Netherlands, which serves as the Regional Internet Registry with authority to delegate Internet Number Resources in its service region.

RIPE Policy: Policy relating to Internet Number Resources developed, adopted, and published by the RIPE community in accordance with the Policy Development Process described at https://www.ripe.net.

Service Agreement: The Standard Service Agreement between the Provider and the RIPE NCC under which the Provider operates as a Local Internet Registry.

Article 2 - General Provisions

2.1 This Agreement shall come into effect upon:

(a) Execution of this Agreement by both parties; and

(b) Receipt by the Provider of an extract from the Commercial Trade Register or equivalent document proving the registration of the End User's business with their national authorities.

If the End User's business has not been incorporated or registered with the Commercial Trade Register, the End User shall instead provide a legible copy of a valid government-issued identity document.

2.2 Upon receipt of the signed Agreement and supporting documents, the Provider shall issue an invoice for the Administration Fee as specified in Schedule A.

2.3 The Provider reserves the right to amend and/or supplement the terms of this Agreement. The Provider shall notify the End User and the RIPE NCC of any changes at least thirty (30) days prior to such amendment or supplement coming into effect. The End User's continued use of the Internet Number Resources after such notice period constitutes acceptance of the amended terms.

2.4 Communication under this Agreement shall be conducted via the email addresses provided by each party. The End User is responsible for ensuring their contact information remains current.

Article 3 - Request for Assignment

3.1 The End User may request an Assignment by contacting the Provider through the designated support channels. Upon receipt of a valid request, the Provider shall submit a request form via the LIR Portal to the RIPE NCC.

3.2 The Provider may suspend processing of an initial Assignment request until:

(a) The Administration Fee specified in paragraph 2.2 has been received; and

(b) The Maintenance Fee specified in paragraph 4.2 for the current billing period has been received.

3.3 The Assignment is made by the RIPE NCC. The following conditions must be met before the RIPE NCC will assign Independent Internet Number Resources:

(a) The Provider has submitted a valid request form to the RIPE NCC;

(b) The RIPE NCC has established that the request complies with current RIPE policies for Assignment to End Users.

Current RIPE documents are available at: https://www.ripe.net/publications/docs/

3.4 The Provider does not guarantee approval of any Assignment request. The RIPE NCC has sole discretion over whether an Assignment complies with applicable policies.

Article 4 - Maintenance; Conditions for Maintenance

4.1 Subject to the provisions of this Article, the Provider shall maintain the Assignments made by the RIPE NCC pursuant to this Agreement.

4.2 The End User shall pay a periodic Maintenance Fee as specified in Schedule A. The Provider may amend the Maintenance Fee during the term of the Agreement, with such amendment to be communicated at least sixty (60) days prior to taking effect.

4.3 The End User understands and agrees that the Provider can only maintain Assignments as long as the assigned Independent Internet Number Resources are used in accordance with RIPE policies. Specifically, the Provider shall maintain Assignments subject to the following conditions:

(a) Internal Use Only: The End User shall use the Independent Internet Number Resources assigned to it for internal purposes within its own network only.

(b) No Proprietary Rights: The End User understands and agrees that the Assignment does not confer upon the End User any proprietary or transferable rights in respect of the Independent Internet Number Resources. The End User shall not assign, delegate, sub-delegate, sell, lease, or otherwise allow third parties to use the Independent Internet Number Resources assigned to it pursuant to this Agreement.

(c) Purpose Limitation: The End User shall use the assigned Independent Internet Number Resources solely for the purpose specified in the request on the basis of which the Independent Internet Number Resources have been assigned.

(d) Policy Compliance: The End User shall comply with current RIPE policies relevant to End Users as published at https://www.ripe.net, which may be amended from time to time by the RIPE community.

(e) Accurate Registration: The End User shall provide the Provider with correct and up-to-date information for recording of the Assignment in the RIPE Database during the entire term of this Agreement.

(f) Correspondence: The End User shall respond to correspondence from the Provider and/or the RIPE NCC regarding Assignments made pursuant to this Agreement within a reasonable timeframe, not to exceed thirty (30) days.

4.4 The End User understands and agrees that the RIPE NCC may revoke Assignments if the End User does not use the assigned Independent Internet Number Resources in accordance with RIPE policies as specified in paragraph 4.3.

4.5 The Provider shall maintain appropriate records of all Assignments and make such records available to the RIPE NCC upon request for audit or compliance purposes.

Article 5 - Payment

5.1 The End User shall pay the Administration Fee and Maintenance Fee within thirty (30) days of the invoice date. The End User shall be in default immediately upon failure to pay within this period, with no notice of default being required.

5.2 All fees are payable in {{currency}}. Payment may be made via:

  • Bitcoin Lightning Network
  • Bank transfer
  • Other payment methods as may be specified by the Provider

5.3 With effect from the day on which the End User defaults on its payment obligations, the End User shall owe the Provider:

(a) Interest at the statutory commercial rate on the amounts unpaid; and

(b) All reasonable collection costs incurred by the Provider.

5.4 Failure to pay the Maintenance Fee may result in suspension of services and, ultimately, termination of this Agreement and revocation of the Assignment.

Article 6 - Liability

6.1 The Provider does not warrant that requested Independent Internet Number Resources will be assigned upon request.

6.2 The Provider does not warrant that assigned Independent Internet Number Resources will be routable on any part of the Internet. Routability depends on third-party network operators over whom the Provider has no control.

6.3 The End User shall be solely liable for all aspects of the use of the Independent Internet Number Resources assigned to it and all consequences arising from such use.

6.4 The Provider excludes all liability for any direct or indirect damages, including but not limited to:

  • Loss of business or profits
  • Damages to third parties
  • Personal injury or property damage
  • Consequential or incidental damages

This exclusion does not apply in cases of wilful misconduct or gross negligence on the part of the Provider or its management.

6.5 The Provider shall not be liable for damages caused by:

(a) Failure by the RIPE NCC to make Independent Internet Number Resources available or to do so in a timely manner;

(b) Any act or omission of the RIPE NCC;

(c) Changes to RIPE policy affecting the End User's use of the resources.

6.6 The Provider shall not be liable for non-performance or damages where such is not due to the Provider's fault or is due to circumstances beyond the Provider's reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, war, terrorism, civil unrest, or failure of third-party infrastructure.

6.7 The End User shall indemnify and hold harmless the Provider against any and all third-party claims, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising from or related to the End User's use of the Independent Internet Number Resources assigned pursuant to this Agreement.

6.8 In all cases, the Provider's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed the total amount of fees actually paid by the End User to the Provider under this Agreement during the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to liability.

Article 7 - Term and Termination

7.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an indefinite period until terminated in accordance with this Article 7.

7.2 Termination by End User: The End User may terminate this Agreement with one (1) month's written notice sent to the Provider by email or registered mail.

7.3 Termination by Provider: The Provider may terminate this Agreement with three (3) months' written notice sent to the End User by email or registered mail.

7.4 Termination for Cause: The Provider may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to the End User, without liability and without prejudice to any other remedies, if:

(a) The End User fails to comply with paragraphs 4.3(a), (b), (c), or (d);

(b) The End User fails to fulfil any other obligation under this Agreement and fails to remedy such failure within fourteen (14) days of receiving written notice;

(c) The End User violates any applicable law in a manner that, in the Provider's reasonable opinion, justifies immediate termination;

(d) Bankruptcy, insolvency, or similar proceedings are filed by or against the End User;

(e) The End User goes into liquidation or becomes insolvent;

(f) The End User uses the Internet Number Resources in connection with illegal activities, including but not limited to spamming, distribution of malware, or cyberattacks.

7.5 Effect on Fees: Termination shall not affect Administration or Maintenance Fees that have become due or have been paid prior to the date of termination. No refunds shall be provided for prepaid fees.

7.6 Post-Termination Actions: Upon termination:

(a) The Provider shall request the RIPE NCC to delete the RIPE Database records for the Independent Internet Number Resources assigned pursuant to this Agreement;

(b) The Provider shall request the RIPE NCC to revoke Reverse DNS delegations for the respective resources;

(c) The Provider and RIPE NCC may take any other measures necessary to enable the Independent Internet Number Resources to become eligible for reassignment.

7.7 Cessation of Use: Upon termination, and except for replacement of this Agreement by another agreement for the same Independent Internet Number Resources as described in paragraph 7.9:

(a) The End User shall immediately cease all use of the Independent Internet Number Resources;

(b) The resources may be reassigned by the RIPE NCC to other End Users;

(c) The End User waives any claim against the Provider or the RIPE NCC for continued use of the resources.

7.8 Automatic Expiry: This Agreement shall automatically expire upon:

(a) Declaration of bankruptcy of the Provider;

(b) Liquidation of the Provider; or

(c) Termination of the Service Agreement between the Provider and the RIPE NCC.

7.9 Continuity Options: After termination under paragraphs 7.2, 7.3, or expiry under paragraph 7.8, the End User may seek to continue using the same Independent Internet Number Resources by:

(a) Entering into an End User Assignment Agreement directly with the RIPE NCC; or

(b) Entering into an agreement with another LIR.

The End User acknowledges that the Independent Internet Number Resources may be reassigned if the End User does not arrange alternative sponsorship within three (3) months of termination.

9.2 Except as expressly stated in paragraph 9.1, this Agreement does not create any rights in favour of any third party, and no third party shall have the right to enforce any term of this Agreement.

Article 10 - Miscellaneous

10.1 Assignment: The End User may not assign any rights or obligations under this Agreement without the Provider's prior written consent.

10.2 Disclosure to RIPE NCC: The Provider shall submit to the RIPE NCC copies of this Agreement and supporting documents as required for verifying the status of the Independent Internet Number Resources and compliance with RIPE policies.

10.3 Address Changes: The End User shall notify the Provider immediately of any change of address, email, or billing details. Until such notification, the last notified details shall be presumed correct.

10.4 Notices: Unless otherwise provided, the Provider may send notifications under this Agreement to the last notified email and mail address of the End User.

10.5 Severability: If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

10.6 Entire Agreement: This Agreement, together with any schedules and annexes, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties concerning its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions.

10.7 Amendments: Except as provided in paragraphs 2.3 and 4.2, this Agreement may only be amended by written agreement signed by both parties.

10.8 Waiver: No failure or delay by either party in exercising any right under this Agreement shall constitute a waiver of that right.

10.9 Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement if such failure results from circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to natural disasters, war, terrorism, labour disputes, or failure of third-party telecommunications.
